About me

Currently enrolled in the PhD program at the Department of Mathematics, at Florida state university, I am interested in the applications of LÃ©vy processes in Finance. My previous education in France and in the United States, as well as my research and work experience with Citigroup, Rothschild and Fortis Investment, drive a strong will to develop robust tools and bring appropriate solutions to both practitioners and academics.

My research focus is on numerial methods for partial differential equations and stochastic analysis. I have been working in the fields of stochastic analysis, partial differential equations, numerical analysis, credit derivatives, option pricing, spectral element methods and finite differences, and am currently teaching FORTRAN and C++ for students in the Masters of Applied and Financial Mathematics.

Current research projects in C++ include mesh construction problems, binomial trees, spectral element and finite differences solvers, fast Fourier transform methods for option pricing and Monte Carlo. Past projects include the integration of CDS pricing models and portfolio optimization in VBA/Bloomberg environment, and the development of financial libraries in an HPC architecture.
